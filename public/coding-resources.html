The paywalls that have gone up around coding resources over the past 5 years irritate me. It is not the spirit of the web or coding. Coders are built on the help they have received - for free - from others. Open source is a founding concept behind coding and the programming community. Stack overflow IS the community, helping each other out for free. Barriers to entry in programming is a concern to me in an industry that is already full of stereotypes, creating systems with coded bias that everyone relies upon. It is also, first and foremost, a hobby that everyone with access to a computer should be able to enjoy. Do not feel the need to pay for content, there are so many resources out there that are free to use, and help whereever you turn. Of course, this is not to say that you should not a) donate / support your favourite creators through sites such as patreon b) be generous with your own time in the future. Remember how much you benefitted from the intellectual generoisty of others. Programming is inherently collaboartive. No one codes alone. Youre either looking to others for help and support or youre using tools that have been crafted by others before you. Even the language you choose to use. Foundation founding bedrock.
